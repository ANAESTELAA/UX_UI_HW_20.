SLAM

SLAM stands for Salt Lake Academy of Music . A non-profit in Utah that delivers online and in person instruction in state-of-the-art studios.

Slam was founded to assist families who have interest in music but also have financial needs,offering a free to fee sliding scale .
Making music available to all.Since I enjoy music I found this very interesting.

The Problem

Many parents are looking to involve their kids into music school that offers low cost and that has excellent reputation.


However its hard for them to trust a website to get all their information from.Slam offers this but the website is cluttered, making it hard for the parents to decide if it’s a good fit for them and making a choice at first glance.

The Solution

Analyzing the website had all the information for the user but it was all over the place .


It needed some designer assistance to make it more appealing and easier to navigate.As UX designers our solution was to aim to make a better use experience not only to benefit the user but also for SLAMS benefit.

Interviews

We conducted user interviews to help me better understand who would use the SLAM website, and what they would use it for.


When selecting people to interview, we looked for parents whose kids are interested in learning music and asked them about what they would look for in a music school.

We hypothesized that our proto-persona would be Martin, a businessman whose son is interested in learning music and starting a band.

From that we came to a conclusion that our persona was Audrey.

Audrey is a part time teacher within a child who is interested in music but with a tight budget she's looking for a musical school that will fulfill her child's musical needs and her budget.

Card Sorting

Card sorting helped discover that the Navigation on the slam website was a bit too busy and the whole design needed more attention to the needs of the user.Within their broad categories I decided to made subcategories to make a more pleasant navigation for my user.

When conducting the heuristic evaluation I understood that we needed to make a cleaner design to help the user focus on the most valuable things that SLAM has to offer.

Site Maping

The site map was crucial in organizing and structuring the content and features of the website in a logical and intuitive manner.


We took Audrey's requirements and motivations into careful consideration, ensuring that the site map aligned with her goals of finding suitable music programs for her children and understanding the advantages of choosing Slam.

Style Guide

Navigation

We streamlined the navigation menu by condensing it and grouping related items together.


This helped to declutter the navigation, making it easier for users to find what they're looking for.

Card

While having a the contact form is important in the original design the page would pop up on every page and it was inconsistent.

We created a designated page for the 'Contact Us'card and made it cleaner and more visually appealing.

Mid-fi Prototype

Our mid-fi prototype played a crucial role in validating design decisions, incorporating user feedback, and refining the user experience.

This prototype paved the way for the development of a high-fidelity prototype that will closely resemble the final product, which is a website that meets Audrey's needs and effectively communicates the benefits of enrolling in Slam.

Usability Testing & Iterations

After conducting usability testing,we discovered that our users were having difficulty finding the STAFF MEMBERS section on the ABOUT page. This feedback guided us to move the STAFF MEMBERS section to the LESSONS page, where it would be more easily accessible and visible to users. As shown here.
